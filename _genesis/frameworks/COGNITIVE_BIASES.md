# COGNITIVE BIASES — The UBS of Human Thinking
### Derived From: Ultimate Truth #6; Wiki — UBS of Human Thinking

---

## Core Principle
The personal UBS = Psychological Biases.
The personal UDS = Logical & Analytical Reasoning.
Guard against the former; strengthen the latter.

---

## The 5 Key Biases to Defend Against

### 1. AVAILABILITY BIAS
**What:** Over-weighting information that is recent, vivid, or emotionally charged.
**Defense:** Ask "What data am I NOT seeing? What happened before the recent event?"

### 2. REPRESENTATIVENESS BIAS
**What:** Assuming a small sample represents the whole population.
**Defense:** Ask "Is my sample size large enough? Am I generalizing from too few examples?"

### 3. ANCHORING BIAS
**What:** Over-weighting the first piece of information encountered.
**Defense:** Ask "Would I reach the same conclusion if I encountered the data in reverse order?"

### 4. AFFECT BIAS
**What:** Letting emotional reactions influence analytical judgments.
**Defense:** Ask "Am I reacting emotionally or analytically? Would I decide differently in a calm state?"

### 5. CONFIRMATION BIAS
**What:** Seeking only information that supports an existing belief.
**Defense:** Ask "What evidence would DISPROVE my hypothesis? Have I actively looked for it?"

## Additional Biases to Watch For

| Bias | Description | Defense |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Survivorship | Ignoring failed cases, only studying successes | Study failures explicitly |
| Sunk-cost fallacy | Continuing failed approaches due to prior investment | Evaluate future value only |
| Dunning-Kruger | Overconfidence in areas of low competence | Seek expert review |
| Status quo | Preferring the current state over change | Evaluate change on its merits |
| Groupthink | Conforming to group consensus without critical evaluation | Assign a devil's advocate |
