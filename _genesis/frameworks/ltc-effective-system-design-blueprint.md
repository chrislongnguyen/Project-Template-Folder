# [COE EFF]_C. EFFECTIVENESS - AREA TRAINING MATERIALS - 2. Effective System Design

### EFFECTIVE SYSTEM DESIGN

In order to achieve the desired outcomes in the System, the doer/user/learner must have an effective system to enable them to perform the right actions. This effective system has 8 fundamental components:

1. **The Effective Inputs (i.e., the System's "EI").** The Effective Inputs include sustainable, efficient and scalable quality AND amount of one or more of the following:
    1. Acceptance Criteria (i.e., sustainability, efficiency and scalability) of this system
    2. Signal or data or information
    3. Energy
    4. Physical Resources
    5. Human Resources
    6. Financial Resources
2. **The Effective Users (i.e, the System's "EU").** There are 4 types of users in any system, and can be categorized using the RACI framework:
    1. Responsible User
    2. Accountable User
    3. Consulted User
    4. Informed User
3. **The Effective User's Effective Actions (i.e, the System's "EA")**
4. **The Effective Outputs** of the Effective User's Effective Actions **(i.e, the System's "EO").** The Effective Outputs include sustainable, efficient and scalable quality AND amount of one or more of the following:
    1. Acceptance Criteria (i.e., sustainability, efficiency and scalability) for the next system
    2. Signal or data or information
    3. Energy
    4. Physical Resources
    5. Human Resources
    6. Financial Resources
5. **The Effective Principles** of the Effective User's Effective Actions **(i.e, the System's "EP").** The Effective Principles are critical to ensure Effective Outputs. It is the foundation on which the system operates. In order to be effective, these principles must perform BOTH of these two goals well:
    1. Minimize failure risks by disable/avoid the main blockers (UBS) and root blockers (the drivers of UBS or the blockers of the UDS)
    2. Optimize the desired output by enable/utilize the main drivers (UDS) and root drivers (the drivers of UDS or the blockers of UBS)
6. **The Effective Operating Environment** of the Effective User's Effective Actions **(i.e, the System's "EOE")**
7. **The Effective Operating Tools** used in the Effective User's Effective Actions **(i.e, the System's "EOT")**
8. **The Effective Operating Procedure** of the Effective User's Effective Actions **(i.e, the System's "EOP")**

The following table provide details of the effective system. It facilitates a comprehensive diagnosis and allows the user to design the effective system to achieve their desired outcomes.
