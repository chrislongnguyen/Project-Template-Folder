# [COE EFF]_C. EFFECTIVENESS - AREA TRAINING MATERIALS - 1. Truths We Need to Master to Succeed

### 1. ULTIMATE TRUTHS WE NEED TO MASTER TO SUCCEED IN LIFE

1. **ULTIMATE TRUTH #1:** Universal truth of systems

We are never doing things by ourselves. We are always a user of a system that includes 8 fundamental components:
    1. **The Users**: Us,
    2. **Our Actions**,
    3. **The Inputs** that the systems require to operate. They include success criteria and resources such as energy, data, human, physical and financial,
    4. **The Outcome/Output** of our Actions,
    5. **The Principles** of our Actions,
    6. **The Operating Environment** of our Actions,
    7. **The Operating Tools** used in our Actions,
    8. **The Operating Procedure** of Our Actions.

2. **ULTIMATE TRUTH #2:** The success criteria of the system can always be simplified into a deliverable that has the following 4 fundamental elements:
    1. **Verb** (for Our Actions)
    2. **Adverbs** of the Verb (The Outcome)
    3. **Noun** (The Tools, The Environment, and implicitly The Principles - sometimes explicitly so)
    4. **Adjectives** of the Noun (the effectiveness of the Noun)

3. **ULTIMATE TRUTH #3:** Why do we need effective principles for our actions to be successful?

Because the outcome of our system is always hindered by a collection of blockers (the Ultimate Blocking System, or the "**UBS**"), and is driven by a collection of drivers (the Ultimate Driving System, or the "**UDS**"). The best and most effective outcome requires the best approach to overcome the UBS and utilize the UDS. Such an approach is described by the effective principles of our actions.

4. **ULTIMATE TRUTH #4:** Success = Optimal Value Creation for a Given Amount of Resources

5. **ULTIMATE TRUTH #5:** Because of the stronger influence of the UBS than the UDS for valuable outcomes (i.e., ones that are worth pursuing in life), success in reality is first about efficient and scalable management of failure risks before maximizing work output.

6. **ULTIMATE TRUTH #6:** For personal success:
    1. The UBS is our system of psychological biases, and
    2. The UDS is our system of logical & analytical reasoning.

7. **ULTIMATE TRUTH #7:** The process of achieving success (efficient & scalable risk management) for humans includes the following 6 work streams:
    1. Self Alignment (choosing the right definition of successful outcome)
    2. Risk Management (minimizing failure risks in every step)
    3. Effective Learning (finding the truths and premises to think)
    4. Effective Thinking (generate possible outcomes)
    5. Effective Decision Making & Planning (prioritizing & choosing outcomes to execute)
    6. Effective Execution (effective process, effective capacity & capability of the doer, effective tools, and effective environment to execute the chosen outcome)

8. **ULTIMATE TRUTH #8:** For team/organizational success:
    1. The UBS is human-related biases (including nature & character flaws that are harmful for team performance, and psychological biases that are harmful for individuals to see the truths). These human-related biases lead to the existence of unofficial/shadow operating systems in teams or organizations. The unofficial systems, unfortunately, are the ultimate ones deciding the true performance of their team/organization.
    2. The UDS is the collective logical & analytical reasoning capability of the organization

9. **ULTIMATE TRUTH #9:** The process of achieving success of a team or an organization includes the following 6 work streams:
    1. Team/Organization Alignment (choosing the right definition of successful outcome)
    2. Risk Management (minimizing failure risks in every step)
    3. Effective Learning (finding the truths and premises to think)
    4. Effective Thinking (generate possible outcomes)
    5. Effective Decision Making & Planning (prioritizing & choosing outcomes to execute)
    6. Effective Execution (effective process, effective capacity & capability of the doers, effective tools, and effective environment to execute the chosen outcome)

    An important application of organization success is effective product development, in which design thinking is the process that involves team(s) of human.

10. **ULTIMATE TRUTH #10:** The process of achieving success must include work streams, not phases, stages or steps because life is highly uncertain. The most effective approach to deal with uncertainty and achieve the best possible outcome is to do these 6 work streams concurrently, not sequentially.

This is the basis of agile principles, which include continuous value creation (i.e., delivery) and continuous improvement.

### 2. DERIVED TRUTHS WE NEED TO MASTER TO SUCCEED IN LIFE

1. **DERIVED TRUTH #1:** Key aspects of effectiveness and success - Derived from Ultimate Truths #5

Since success requires first efficient and scalable of failure risks, then efficient and scalable production of outputs, success depend on three fundamental pillars in this order:
    1. Sustainability (i.e., management of failure risks)
    2. Efficiency (best possible output level for a given amount of resources)
    3. Scalability (best possible output gains for a given increase in resources)

Being efficient is not enough for success. Success requires effectiveness because it consists of all three pillars of success.

2. **DERIVED TRUTH #2:** Derived from Ultimate Truths #2

Success must always include the three fundamental pillars of sustainability, efficiency and scalability. Therefore, success criteria of everything can be described as the following:
    1. **Verb and Adverb** (for the Users' Actions):
        1. Minimize Failure Risks sustainably, efficiently and scalably
        2. Maximize the Desired Outcomes/Output sustainably, efficiently and scalably
    2. **Noun and Adjectives** (for the Principles, Inputs, Environment, and Tools)
        1. Sustainable, efficient and scalable Principles, Inputs, Environment, and Tools.

3. **DERIVED TRUTH #3:** The three pillars of effectiveness are also true for all 6 works streams:
    1. Effective Alignment must be sustainable, efficient and scalable
    2. Effective Learning must be sustainable, efficient and scalable
    3. Effective Thinking must be sustainable, efficient and scalable
    4. Effective Decision Making & Planning must be sustainable, efficient and scalable
    5. Effective Execution must be sustainable, efficient and scalable
