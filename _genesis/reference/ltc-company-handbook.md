# 1. INTRODUCTION TO LONG TERM CAPITAL PARTNERS



# 1.1. Our History

### **OUR HISTORY**

Founded in 2015, Long Term Capital Partners ("LTC") initially operated as a private family office, managing the personal assets of our founders and close associates. Over the past decade, we've significantly expanded our scope, now serving a diverse clientele that includes multiple families and institutional investors. This growth led to the establishment of dedicated teams specializing in **wealth management**, **corporate structuring & development**, and **philanthropic advisory**.

Today, LTC proudly manages over **$100 million in assets**. Our headquarter is located in **Ho Chi Minh City**, with an additional representative presence in **Singapore**.

# 3.2. Our Three Pillars of Success (Our Three Pillars of Effectiveness)

### OUR PHILOSOPHY OF SUCCESS

At Long Term Capital Partners, we believe that **the most certain way to achieve Success is via Effectiveness**. However, our definition of effectiveness diverges from the conventional business obsession with merely maximizing output.
While the conventional belief of effectiveness is equivalent to maximizing output for current and future resources, it assumes a predictable world. In reality, life and markets are governed by profound uncertainty. We recognize that for every single path to success, there are countless failure paths waiting to be taken. Therefore, true effectiveness is not just about how fast we move, but how effectively we navigate away from those failure paths.

We believe that **Effectiveness is the ability to manage risks in an efficient and scalable way.**

We view our operations through a defensive lens: to win, we must first ensure we do not lose. This philosophy of risk management is structured across our three pillars:

#### Pillar 1: Sustainability
Definition: The Management of Failure Risks. Sustainability is our foundation. It is not merely about environmental consciousness or long-term planning; it is the fundamental management of failure risks to ensure survival.
*   In a world where failure paths are prevalent, our primary goal is to identify, mitigate, and eliminate the risks that could threaten the firm’s existence or our clients' legacies.
*   If a process or strategy is not sustainable—meaning it exposes us to ruinous risk—it is ineffective, regardless of its short-term returns.

#### Pillar 2: Efficiency
Definition: The Optimal Output of De-risking at Current Resources. Efficiency is how we execute our defense today. It is defined as the optimal output of de-risking we can achieve using our currently available resources (time, capital, and talent).
*   We do not measure efficiency solely by "speed of completion." We measure it by "cost of safety."
*   To be efficient means to identify and neutralize risks with the least amount of waste and effort possible.

#### Pillar 3: Scalability
Definition: The Optimal Gain in De-risking relative to Resource Growth. Scalability is how we prepare for tomorrow. It is defined as the optimal output gain in de-risking relative to our rate of increase in resources.
*   As LTC adds new families, assets, and team members, complexity increases. A scalable organization ensures that our ability to manage risk grows _faster_ than our complexity.
*   If we double our assets (resources), our ability to protect those assets (de-risking) should more than double. If our risk exposure grows faster than our resources, we are not scaling; we are becoming fragile.

We foster a culture of **continuous learning**, believing that growth never stops. *   **Strategic Focus Area 2: Operational Excellence (OE)**
    
    We believe in working smarter. Our focus on **lean processes** means we constantly optimize our workflows, eliminating waste and maximizing efficiency. We strategically **leverage smart technology** to enhance our capabilities, and we're driven by a commitment to **relentless improvement** in every aspect of our operations.
    

*   **Strategic Focus Area 3: User Enablement (UE) (Formerly Product Leadership PL)**
    
    We have evolved this focus area from "Product Leadership" to "User Enablement" to better align with our mission of being the "extended minds and hands" of our clients. In a complex financial world, we recognize that value is not defined by the complexity of a product, but by the **capability it creates for the user**. This means providing **actionable insights** rather than just data, developing **intuitive tools** that reduce the "cost of safety" for our internal teams, and ensuring that every solution empowers the user to **confidently navigate risk** and seize opportunities.

    Building deep, trusted relationships is paramount. We achieve this through **proactive communication**, ensuring you're always informed and engaged. Our **bespoke advisory services** are tailored precisely to your unique situation, and we strive to create genuine **delight through partnership**, making your experience with LTC truly exceptional.

    Ultimately, our success is measured by the value we create. We are dedicated to delivering **sustainable long-term value** for both our clients and the firm itself. This isn't just about financial metrics; it's about safeguarding and growing wealth across generations, ensuring a legacy that endures.
    

*   **Strategic Focus Area 6: Impactful Corporate Responsibility (CR)**
    
    We believe that true value creation extends beyond the balance sheet. Our commitment to **Corporate Responsibility** reflects our duty to act as stewards not just for our clients' wealth, but for our community, our environment, and the broader society. We strive to maintain our l**icense to operate** by upholding the highest standards of **ethical conduct**, **transparency**, and **social impact**. This focus area ensures that as we grow, we remain a positive force for all our stakeholders.

# 6. HOW DO WE USE/ALLOCATE OUR RESOURCES?

Our approach to resource allocation is disciplined and directly aligned with our seven strategic focus areas. We believe that how we deploy our capital, people, and time is the ultimate expression of our strategy.

Every allocation decision is weighed against its potential to enhance People Development, drive Operational Excellence, secure Product Leadership, deepen Customer Intimacy, deliver Outstanding Financial Performance, and ensure Impactful Corporate Responsibility.

# 6.1. How Do We Use/Allocate Our Human Resources?

### A SKILLS-BASED, "FLOW-TO-WORK" MODEL

At Long Term Capital Partners, we do not view human, our most valuable resources, through the lens of static job titles or rigid departments. Instead, we operate as a dynamic, skills-based organization inspired by the Scaled Agile Framework.

In this model, the Center of Excellence (COE) is the "real company, i.e., the stable home where all members reside as experts. The Functional Groups are the vehicles through which value is delivered.

*   **1\. THE HOME BASE: THE CENTER OF EXCELLENCE (COE)**
    
    Every member of LTC is, first and foremost, a member of the Center of Excellence. This is our "home base."
    
    *   Role: The COE is responsible for the recruitment, career stability, and continuous capability building of our people. It is where you hone your craft within our eight areas of excellence (e.g., Investment, Tech, Data Science, Leadership).
    *   Goal: To ensure Sustainability of our talent pool and People Development (Focus Area 1), ensuring that regardless of the project you are on, your professional growth is constant and supported.
    

  

*   **2\. THE DEPLOYMENT: FLOWING TO WORK IN RELEVANT FUNCTIONAL GROUPS**
    
    We do not hoard talent in silos. Instead, our experts "flow to the work". Depending on strategic priorities, members from the COE are dynamically deployed into cross-functional Agile Teams within our four Functional Groups. A single member may hold multiple roles across different projects, maximizing Efficiency and resource utilization.
    
    *   Client Fulfillment (FULFILL):
        *   When a complex deal or client need arises, we assemble teams comprising investment experts (from COE INV), legal minds (from COE BIZ), and tech specialists (from COE TECH) to execute within functions like Business Ventures (BIZ VEN) or Client Services (CLIENT SERV).
        *   _Purpose:_ Delivering Product Leadership and Customer Intimacy.
    *   Strategic Growth (GROW):
        *   To capture new market opportunities, we deploy persuasive communicators and strategists to the Business Development (BIZ DEV) function.
        *   _Purpose:_ Driving Outstanding Financial Performance.
    *   Innovation & Continuous Improvement (INNO):
        *   When internal systems need upgrading or new proprietary tools are required, developers and process architects flow into Investment Tech (INV TECH) or Business Tech (BIZ TECH).
        *   _Purpose:_ Ensuring Scalability through technology.
    *   The Engine Room (OPERATIONS):
        *   To maintain stability, operational experts deploy into Management (MGT), HR, and Finance roles. While these roles are often more permanent, the "flow to work" mindset remains—resources shift to where the operational load is heaviest.
        *   _Purpose:_ Securing Operational Excellence and Strategic Alignment.
    

*   **3\. PROJECT MANAGEMENT VS. PEOPLE MANAGEMENT**
    *   Functions are for Project Management: The Heads of GROW, FULFILL, INNO, and OPS manage the _work_, the _deadlines_, and the _outcomes_.
    *   The COE is for People Management: The COE Leads ensure the _people_ are skilled, happy, and developing.
    

This structure allows LTC to remain agile. We can rapidly spin up a team to tackle a new venture or solve a crisis, and dissolve it just as quickly when the work is done, returning those experts to the COE pool for their next mission. Resource allocation is tracked transparently in ClickUp, ensuring that "flowing to work" never leads to burnout, but rather to a diverse and stimulating career.

# 6.2. How Do We Use/Allocate Our Financial Resources?

### FUNDING STABILITY AND AGILITY

Our financial capital is allocated with a disciplined focus on Sustainability and Long-Term Value Creation. We view financial allocation through two distinct lenses: funding our Capabilities (the COE) and funding our Execution (the Functional Teams).

The allocation framework is managed by the Operations Function Group (OPS), specifically the Management (MGT) and Finance (OPS FIN) teams, with oversight from the Board of Directors.

*   **1\. INVESTING IN OUR EXPERTS (The COE - Focus Area 1)**
    
    We treat our human capital as a long-term asset, not a variable project cost.
    
    *   **Talent Stability:** A significant portion of our fixed budget is allocated to the Center of Excellence (COE). This covers competitive compensation and benefits for all members, regardless of their current project assignment
    *   **Continuous Growth:** We dedicate specific funds to the COE for learning and development initiatives. This ensures that our "expert pool" is constantly appreciating in value, directly supporting People Development.
    

*   **2\. OPTIMIZING THE PLATFORM (The Machine - Focus Area 2)**
    
    We invest in the infrastructure that allows our teams to flow and collaborate effectively.
    
    *   **System Scalability:** We allocate capital to the Innovation (INNO) and Operations (OPS) functions to maintain and upgrade our core technology stack (ClickUp, Google Workspace, Odoo). This investment ensures that our systems remain scalable and efficient as the firm grows.
    

*   **3\. FUELING STRATEGIC INITIATIVES (The Work - Focus Areas 3, 4, & 5)**
    
    We allocate variable capital to the Fulfillment (FULFILL) and Growth (GROW) functions based on strategic priority and ROI.
    
    *   **Dynamic Project Budgeting:** Rather than rigid departmental budgets, capital flows to specific project teams (e.g., a Venture Launch team or a Fundraising team). This allows us to pivot financial resources quickly to high-impact opportunities.
    *   **Growth Capital:** We allocate aggressive funding to Business Development (BIZ DEV) and Business Ventures (BIZ VEN) to acquire new clients and launch new businesses, driving Outstanding Financial Performance.
    

*   **4\. FULFILLING OUR PHILANTHROPIC COMMITMENT (Focus Area 6)**
    
    In line with our values and our commitment to Impactful Corporate Responsibility, we adhere to a strict financial discipline regarding giving back.
    
    *   Philanthropic Allocation: We formally allocate at least 10% of our annual profits to philanthropic causes. This capital is managed to create meaningful social impact, ensuring our financial success benefits the wider community.

# 6.3. How Do We Use/Allocate Our Time Resources?

### BALANCING DEPLOYMENT AND DEVELOPMENT

Time is our most finite resource. In a "Flow-to-Work" organization, managing capacity is critical to preventing bottlenecks and burnout. We track time rigorously using ClickUp to ensure our collective effort aligns with our strategic OKRs and the three pillars of Effectiveness.

Our resource allocation model distinguishes between **Deployed Time** (working in Agile Teams to deliver value) and **COE Time** (investing in yourself and the firm's capabilities).

*   **1\. 60% TIME DEPLOYED TO CLIENT & GROWTH (The "Work")**
    *   Context: This is when experts flow from the COE into specific Agile Squads within the Fulfillment (FULFILL) and Growth (GROW) functions.
    *   Focus: Direct value creation. This includes executing deals in Business Ventures, managing portfolios in Public Investment, nurturing relationships in Client Services, or driving expansion in Business Development.
    *   Goal: To drive Outstanding Financial Performance and Customer Intimacy by focusing the majority of our energy on external impact.
    

  

*   **2\. 25% TIME DEPLOYED TO OPERATIONS & INNOVATION (The "Machine")**
    *   Context: This time is allocated to squads within Operations (OPS) and Innovation (INNO).
    *   Focus: Building and oiling the engine. This includes implementing the Odoo ERP, developing proprietary software in INV TECH, or optimizing internal SOPs and workflows.
    *   Goal: To ensure Operational Excellence and Scalability. We invest heavily here to ensure that every hour spent in Client Squads is supported by efficient, automated systems.
    

  

*   **3\. 15% TIME RESERVED FOR COE & PERSONAL GROWTH (The "Home")**
    *   Context: This is non-negotiable "protected time" where members retreat to their home base in the Center of Excellence (COE).
    *   Focus: Capability building and culture. This time is dedicated to training, peer-to-peer mentorship, contributing to the Knowledge Library, and engaging in Wellbeing and Personal Hobbies.
    *   Goal: To ensure People Development and Sustainability. By protecting this time, we prevent skill stagnation and burnout, ensuring our experts remain sharp and motivated for their next squad deployment.
    

**Capacity Planning & Agility:** Because we operate on a "Flow-to-Work" basis, these percentages are not static for every individual every week.
*   Execution Cycles: During a critical deal launch, a member may be 90% deployed to a BIZ VEN squad.
*   Cool-Down Periods: After a major project delivery, that member may shift to 50% COE time to recharge and upskill.
*   Transparency: All allocations are visible in ClickUp's Workload view, allowing the Strategy Steering Committee to dynamically reassign experts to high-priority squads without overloading any single individual.

# 9.3. Organizational Structure

### ORGANIZATIONAL PHILOSOPHY: A DYNAMIC, SKILLS-BASED ECOSYSTEM

Our organizational structure is not a traditional hierarchy. It is a dynamic, agile framework designed with a singular purpose: to empower our people and effectively execute our strategy across our seven key focus areas. As a cross-functional multi-family office, our structure fosters collaboration, innovation, and clear accountability, enabling us to be the extended minds and hands for our clients.

We operate as two core entities —LT Capital Partners and PT Consulting— organized into five distinct Function Groups. Each group plays a specific, vital role in our ecosystem, ensuring we deliver on our promises of **Product Leadership**, **Customer Intimacy**, and **Operational Excellence**.

LTC does not operate as a traditional, rigid hierarchy. Instead, we utilize a "Flow to Work" model inspired by the Scaled Agile Framework. This structure is designed to balance Sustainability (career stability and skill growth) with Efficiency (rapid deployment of talent to strategic priorities).
Our structure distinguishes between where our people "reside" (to grow their skills) and where our people "work" (to deliver value).

#### THE TWO PILLARS OF OUR ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**1\. THE HOME BASE: THE CENTER OF EXCELLENCE (COE)**
*   **Purpose:** To house our talent, ensure career stability, and drive capability building.
*   **Function:** Every member of LTC belongs to a specific area within the COE based on their primary skillset (e.g., Investment, Technology, Data Science).
*   **Management Focus:** COE Leads are responsible for the development of our people capability.

**2\. THE DEPLOYMENT: THE FUNCTIONAL GROUPS**
*   Purpose: To deliver value to clients and the firm through specific projects and strategic initiatives.
*   Function: These are the destinations where COE members are deployed. Work is organized into Agile Squads formed within these groups to execute specific OKRs.
*   Management Focus: Function Heads and Team Leads are responsible for Project Management—deadlines, deliverables, and outcomes.

### **1\. THE HOME BASE: THE CENTER OF EXCELLENCE (COE)**

### **2\. THE DEPLOYMENT: THE FOUR FUNCTIONAL GROUPS**

Its sole function, **Business Development ("BIZ DEV")**, is the forward-looking engine that identifies new opportunities, forges new partnerships, and expands our client base.

            4. **Corporate Development Function ("CORD DEV"):** Manages value-creation activities for LTC, LTC's invested companies or as a service for the firm's clients.

This group is dedicated to enhancing current services and creating new solutions to meet the evolving demands of our clients, directly supporting our **Product Leadership** goals. It includes the following two functions:
    1.     1.     1. **Investment Technology Function ("INV TECH"):** Develops and manages proprietary technology for our investment processes.
            2. **Business Technology Function ("BIZ TECH"):** Implements technology solutions to improve the business operations of our group and the private companies we invested in.
