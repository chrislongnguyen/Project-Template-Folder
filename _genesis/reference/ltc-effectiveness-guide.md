# Part 1: Ultimate Truths

## 1. Ultimate Truths We Need to Master to Succeed in Life

### UT#1: Universal Truth of Systems

We are never doing things by ourselves. We are always a user of a system that includes 8 fundamental components:

| # | Component | Description |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **The Users** | Us |
| 2 | **Our Actions** | What we do within the system |
| 3 | **The Inputs** | What the system requires to operate — includes success criteria and resources (energy, data, human, physical, financial) |
| 4 | **The Outcome/Output** | The results of our Actions |
| 5 | **The Principles** | The principles governing our Actions |
| 6 | **The Operating Environment** | The environment in which our Actions occur |
| 7 | **The Operating Tools** | The tools used in our Actions |
| 8 | **The Operating Procedure** | The procedure of our Actions |

### UT#2: VANA Requirement Grammar

The success criteria of the system can always be simplified into a deliverable that has the following 4 fundamental elements:

| Element | Maps To |
|---------|---------|
| **Verb** | For Our Actions |
| **Adverbs** of the Verb | The Outcome |
| **Noun** | The Tools, The Environment, and implicitly The Principles (sometimes explicitly so) |
| **Adjectives** of the Noun | The effectiveness of the Noun |

### UT#3: UBS/UDS Force Analysis

Why do we need effective principles for our actions to be successful?

Because the outcome of our system is always hindered by a collection of blockers (the **Ultimate Blocking System**, or the **"UBS"**), and is driven by a collection of drivers (the **Ultimate Driving System**, or the **"UDS"**). The best and most effective outcome requires the best approach to overcome the UBS and utilize the UDS. Such an approach is described by the effective principles of our actions.

### UT#4: Success = Optimal Value Creation

**Success = Optimal Value Creation for a Given Amount of Resources**

### UT#5: UBS Stronger -- Risk Management First

Because of the stronger influence of the UBS than the UDS for valuable outcomes (i.e., ones that are worth pursuing in life), success in reality is first about efficient and scalable management of failure risks before maximizing work output.

### UT#6: Personal UBS/UDS

For personal success:

- The **UBS** is our system of psychological biases
- The **UDS** is our system of logical & analytical reasoning

### UT#7: Six Work Streams for Personal Success

The process of achieving success (efficient & scalable risk management) for humans includes the following 6 work streams:

| # | Work Stream | Description |
|---|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Self Alignment** | Choosing the right definition of successful outcome |
| 2 | **Risk Management** | Minimizing failure risks in every step |
| 3 | **Effective Learning** | Finding the truths and premises to think |
| 4 | **Effective Thinking** | Generate possible outcomes |
| 5 | **Effective Decision Making & Planning** | Prioritizing & choosing outcomes to execute |
| 6 | **Effective Execution** | Effective process, effective capacity & capability of the doer, effective tools, and effective environment to execute the chosen outcome |

### UT#8: Team/Organizational UBS/UDS

For team/organizational success:

- The **UBS** is human-related biases (including nature & character flaws that are harmful for team performance, and psychological biases that are harmful for individuals to see the truths). These human-related biases lead to the existence of unofficial/shadow operating systems in teams or organizations. The unofficial systems, unfortunately, are the ultimate ones deciding the true performance of their team/organization.
- The **UDS** is the collective logical & analytical reasoning capability of the organization.

### UT#9: Six Work Streams for Organizational Success

The process of achieving success of a team or an organization includes the following 6 work streams:

| # | Work Stream | Description |
|---|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | **Team/Organization Alignment** | Choosing the right definition of successful outcome |
| 2 | **Risk Management** | Minimizing failure risks in every step |
| 3 | **Effective Learning** | Finding the truths and premises to think |
| 4 | **Effective Thinking** | Generate possible outcomes |
| 5 | **Effective Decision Making & Planning** | Prioritizing & choosing outcomes to execute |
| 6 | **Effective Execution** | Effective process, effective capacity & capability of the doers, effective tools, and effective environment to execute the chosen outcome |

An important application of organization success is effective product development, in which design thinking is the process that involves team(s) of human.

### UT#10: Work Streams, Not Phases -- Basis of Agile

The process of achieving success must include **work streams, not phases, stages or steps** because life is highly uncertain. The most effective approach to deal with uncertainty and achieve the best possible outcome is to do these 6 work streams **concurrently, not sequentially**.

This is the basis of agile principles, which include continuous value creation (i.e., delivery) and continuous improvement.

## 2. Derived Truths We Need to Master to Succeed in Life

### DT#1: Three Pillars of Effectiveness (Derived from UT#5)

Since success requires first efficient and scalable management of failure risks, then efficient and scalable production of outputs, success depends on three fundamental pillars **in this order**:

| Priority | Pillar | Definition |
|----------|--------|------------|
| 1 | **Sustainability** | Management of failure risks |
| 2 | **Efficiency** | Best possible output level for a given amount of resources |
| 3 | **Scalability** | Best possible output gains for a given increase in resources |

Being efficient is not enough for success. Success requires **effectiveness** because it consists of all three pillars of success.

### DT#2: VANA Applied to Three Pillars (Derived from UT#2)

Success must always include the three fundamental pillars of sustainability, efficiency and scalability. Therefore, success criteria of everything can be described as:

**Verb and Adverb** (for the Users' Actions):
- Minimize Failure Risks sustainably, efficiently and scalably
- Maximize the Desired Outcomes/Output sustainably, efficiently and scalably

**Noun and Adjectives** (for the Principles, Inputs, Environment, and Tools):
- Sustainable, efficient and scalable Principles, Inputs, Environment, and Tools

### DT#3: Three Pillars Apply to All Six Work Streams

The three pillars of effectiveness are also true for all 6 work streams:

1. Effective Alignment must be sustainable, efficient and scalable
2. Effective Risk Management must be sustainable, efficient and scalable
3. Effective Learning must be sustainable, efficient and scalable
4. Effective Thinking must be sustainable, efficient and scalable
5. Effective Decision Making & Planning must be sustainable, efficient and scalable
6. Effective Execution must be sustainable, efficient and scalable

# Part 2: Effective System Design (ESD) Template

## Introduction

In order to achieve the desired outcomes in the System, the doer/user/learner must have an effective system to enable them to perform the right actions. This effective system has 8 fundamental components:

| Component | Abbreviation | Description |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Effective Inputs** | EI | Sustainable, efficient and scalable quality AND amount of: acceptance criteria, signal/data/information, energy, physical resources, human resources, financial resources |
| **Effective Users** | EU | 4 types via RACI: Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed |
| **Effective Actions** | EA | The Effective User's Effective Actions |
| **Effective Outputs** | EO | Sustainable, efficient and scalable quality AND amount of: acceptance criteria for next system, signal/data/information, energy, physical resources, human resources, financial resources |
| **Effective Principles** | EP | Foundation on which the system operates. Must: (1) Minimize failure risks by disabling/avoiding UBS and root blockers, (2) Optimize desired output by enabling/utilizing UDS and root drivers |
| **Effective Operating Environment** | EOE | The environment of the Effective User's Effective Actions |
| **Effective Operating Tools** | EOT | The tools used in the Effective User's Effective Actions |
| **Effective Operating Procedure** | EOP | The procedure of the Effective User's Effective Actions |

### Template Key Questions

The following table provides the 7 fundamental aspects and 43 core components of the ESD:

| Aspect | Key Questions |
|--------|---------------|
| **Overview** | What is it? What are the EO? What are the EI? |
| **Success** | How does it work successfully? What ultimately causes success (UDS)? How (mechanism)? What principles, environment, tools? |
| **Failure** | How can it fail? What ultimately causes failure (UBS)? How (mechanism)? What principles, environment, tools? |
| **Advanced** | What to do if it fails? Other questions? Next steps? |
