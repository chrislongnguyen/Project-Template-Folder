# CRITICAL THINKING MODEL
### Derived From: Wiki — Critical Thinking; Effective Learning

---

## Structure: Hypothesis + Premises + Reasoning → Conclusion

### 1. Hypothesis (The Problem Statement)
- **Situation:** Who does what to whom, when, where, how, why?
- **Complication:** What breaks, fails, or is unknown?

### 2. Premises (The Evidence)
Distinguish clearly between:
- **Truths** — Verified, confirmed facts
- **Assumptions** — Premises that need testing (each is a risk if wrong)
- **Facts** — Observations that are true but may not be complete

### 3. Reasoning Method
Choose the strongest method available:

| Method | Direction | Strength | When to Use |
|--------|-----------|----------|------------|
| **Deduction** | General truth → Specific conclusion | STRONGEST | When you have verified general principles |
| **Abduction** | Observations → Best explanation | MODERATE | When you need to explain what you see |
| **Induction** | Observations → Generalization | WEAKEST | When you have many examples but no principle |

**Preference order:** Deduction > Abduction > Induction

### 4. Conclusion & Decision
- What did we learn?
- What do we do now?
- What assumptions need further validation?

### 5. Bias Check (Always)
- **Availability bias:** Am I over-weighting recent/vivid information?
- **Representativeness bias:** Am I assuming a small sample represents the whole?
- **Anchoring bias:** Am I over-weighting the first data point?
- **Affect bias:** Am I letting emotions influence my analysis?
- **Confirmation bias:** Did I seek disconfirming evidence?
